# Marketing Glossary

A comprehensive reference of marketing and business terminology used across realtor.com's marketing operations, analytics, and product teams.

## Core Business Concepts

### Two-Sided Marketplace
Our business model that serves two distinct customer groups:
- **Consumers**: Individuals interested in buying, selling, or renting a home. Marketing efforts target these users.
- **Clients**: Real estate agents, brokerages, and Realtors who use our service to connect with Consumers (formerly known as Customers).
- **The Connection**: Revenue is generated by successfully connecting motivated Consumers with Clients through closed deals.

### Seasonal Trends
Predictable performance fluctuations throughout the year:
- **Slower Periods**: Winter months and major holidays typically see reduced activity
- **Peak Seasons**: Spring and summer are busiest times for buying, selling, and moving

### Macroeconomic Trends
Broader economic conditions that impact real estate activity:
- **Key Indicators**: Mortgage interest rates, consumer confidence, economic uncertainty
- **Impact**: These factors significantly affect decisions to move

### Competitive Activity
Market dynamics that affect lead generation:
- **Market Share**: Competition for a finite pool of potential consumers
- **Impact**: Competitive spend can affect costs and lead volume

## Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

### Spend
**Definition**: Total monetary amount invested in advertising campaigns across all paid channels.  
**Usage**: The cost incurred to generate traffic and leads.  
**Aliases**: Advertising Spend, Ad Spend, Marketing Cost

### Lead
**Definition**: A unique event where a consumer submits their personally identifiable information (PII) to buy, sell, or rent a home.  
- **Unique Lead Submitter (ULS)** (alias: lead): A unique consumer who has submitted at least one lead
- **Lead Submission** (alias: inquiry, lead): A unique event where a consumer shares their personally identifiable information (PII) via an online form or over the phone to express interest to buy, sell, or rent a home. A single unique user can submit more than one lead on different listings or for different lines of business (buy, sell, rent)
- **Lead Delivery** (alias: lead): A unique event where a Lead is delivered via RDC's lead routing systems (ALA/GLA) to a client contract to fulfill an RDC client contract. A lead can be delivered to more than one client contract in some cases

**Context**: Each lead represents a potential transaction opportunity.

### EFR (Expected Future Revenue)
**Definition**: The expected revenue generated by a lead submission.  
**Usage**: The North Star metric for performance tracking. Currently on track in Q4 2025.  
**Aliases**: Expected Future Revenue  
**Formula**: Calculated based on lead quality and historical conversion rates  
**Note**: For detailed EFR methodology and modeling, refer to the EFR knowledge base (in development)

### ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)
**Definition**: Key performance indicator measuring campaign profitability.  
**Formula**: `ROAS = EFR / Spend`  
**Interpretation**:
- ROAS > 1: Campaigns generate more revenue than they cost
- ROAS = 1: Campaigns break even
- ROAS < 1: Campaigns lose money
**Note**: Always calculate ROAS using EFR before using other revenue columns

### RPL (Revenue Per Lead)
**Definition**: Average expected future revenue generated per lead.  
**Formula**: `RPL = Sum(EFR) / Sum(LEADS)`  
**Usage**: Measure of lead value

### CPL (Cost Per Lead)
**Definition**: Average cost incurred to acquire each lead.  
**Formula**: `CPL = Sum(Spend) / Sum(LEADS)`  
**Usage**: Measure of cost efficiency in lead acquisition  
**Best Practice**: Use NULLIF to prevent division by zero

### CPC (Cost Per Click)
**Definition**: Average cost incurred for each click on an ad.  
**Formula**: `CPC = Sum(Spend) / Sum(Clicks)`  
**Usage**: Measure of cost efficiency in driving traffic

### LSR (Lead Submission Rate)
**Definition**: Rate at which users who click on an ad convert into a lead.  
**Formula**: `LSR = Sum(Leads) / Sum(Clicks)`  
**Usage**: Measure of conversion from click to lead  
**Also Known As**: Click-to-lead conversion rate

### Listing Price
**Definition**: The value of the property associated with a lead, or the cost to acquire a lead.  
**Context**: Can refer to both property list price or acquisition cost (CPL)  
**Calculation Methods**:
- **Median Listing Price**: Preferred for skewed distributions (use with luxury properties)
- **Mean Listing Price**: Used when distribution is normal or total value matters
- **Volume-Weighted Listing Price**: Used when comparing campaigns with different volumes

### Good Quality Ratio
**Definition**: Proportion of sell-intent leads classified as "good quality sell leads".  
**Formula**: `Good Quality Ratio = Sum(GQ_SELL_LEADS) / Sum(SELL_INTENT_LEADS)`  
**Usage**: Measures lead quality for seller-focused campaigns

### Sell Leads Ratio
**Definition**: Proportion of total leads classified as having "sell intent".  
**Formula**: `Sell Leads Ratio = Sum(SELL_INTENT_LEADS) / Sum(LEADS)`  
**Usage**: Tracks balance of buy vs. sell intent in lead mix

### UU (Unique Users)
**Definition**: Key audience growth metric measuring unique visitors.  
**Status**: Currently underperforming YoY targets in Q4 2025

### Median Home Value
**Definition**: Metric tracking high-value traffic quality.  
**Status**: Currently underperforming due to deliberate short-term trade-off where tests optimized for lead volume to drive EFR. Considered **blocked** pending completion of pricing Workstream.

### Conversion Rate
**Definition**: Percentage of users who complete a desired action.  
**Common Types**:
- **Session to Lead**: Overall conversion rate
- **SRP to PDP**: Search results page to property detail page
- **SRP to LDP**: Search results page to listing detail page
- **PDP to Lead**: Property detail page to lead submission
**Benchmarks**:
- SRP → PDP: 15-25%
- PDP → Lead: 3-8%
- Overall session → Lead: 0.5-2%

## Campaign Types & Strategies

### DSA (Dynamic Search Ads)
**Definition**: Google ad type that auto-generates ads based on website content.  
**Usage**: Automated campaign type for broad coverage

### AI Max
**Definition**: Google's newer AI-driven search campaign type focusing on intent discovery with enhanced control over Performance Max.  
**Features**: 
- More granular control (negative keywords, brand exclusions, location targeting)
- Better transparency than Performance Max
- Search-focused rather than full-funnel
- Intent discovery emphasis
**Status**: Initial tests at RDC showed negative results (December 2025)  
**Platform**: Google only (Bing does not offer AI Max)

### Performance Max (PMax)
**Definition**: Google's automated AI-driven campaign type covering all inventory.  
**Features**: 
- Full-funnel optimization across Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Discovery, Maps
- Broader reach but less granular control than AI Max
- Goal-based bidding for conversions
**Platform**: Available on both Google and Bing (Bing's version has different features)  
**Comparison**: Think of AI Max as an advanced, controllable Search tool to find new demand, while PMax is the all-in-one engine for capturing demand across Google's entire network

### Buy Intent Campaigns
**Definition**: Campaigns targeting users with high purchase intent signals.  
**Strategy**: Focus on users showing strong buying behavior indicators

### Brand Campaigns
**Definition**: Campaigns targeting branded search terms.  
**Strategy**: Protect brand traffic and maintain market share

### Land/Lots Campaigns
**Definition**: Campaigns specifically targeting land and lot listings.  
**Context**: Separate strategy from residential property campaigns

### BAU (Business As Usual)
**Definition**: Baseline campaigns used for comparison.  
**Usage**: Reference point for testing and performance evaluation

### VBB (Value-Based Bidding)
**Definition**: Sophisticated bidding strategy to acquire users predicted to be of higher value.  
**Context**: High-risk, high-reward strategy piloted on Homefinder platform

### Dual Serving
**Definition**: Running traffic to two different user experiences simultaneously to test performance.  
**Usage**: A/B testing methodology

## Channel Attribution & Sources

### Paid Search
**Definition**: Google Ads, Bing Ads, and other paid search advertising.  
**Quality Note**: Consistently delivers leads with 30-37% lower median list prices than other channels (expected behavior)

### Organic Search
**Definition**: Unpaid search traffic from search engines.  
**Strategy**: SEO-driven traffic acquisition

### Direct
**Definition**: Direct URL entry or bookmarked traffic.  
**Context**: Indicates brand strength and return visitors

### Referral
**Definition**: Traffic from other websites.  
**Strategy**: Partnership and content distribution channels

### Social
**Definition**: Traffic from social media platforms.  
**Channels**: Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, etc.

### Last-Touch Attribution (Default)
**Definition**: The most recent marketing touchpoint before lead submission gets credit.  
**Usage**: Standard attribution model unless specified otherwise

### Multi-Touch Attribution
**Definition**: Attribution considering multiple touchpoints in the customer journey.  
**Components**:
- **First Touch**: Initial discovery channel
- **Last Touch**: Final conversion channel
- **Assisted Conversions**: Channels in the path

## Geographic & Market Terms

### DMA (Designated Market Area)
**Definition**: TV market regions used for geographic analysis.  
**Usage**: Primary geographic segmentation for marketing

### Market Tiers
**Definition**: Prioritization framework for geographic markets:
- **Tier 1**: Top 20 DMAs by listing inventory, >1000 active listings
- **Tier 2**: 21-50 DMAs, 250-1000 active listings
- **Tier 3**: 51+ DMAs, <250 active listings

### Zero-Lead Markets
**Definition**: Geographic areas with active listings but no lead generation.  
**Investigation Criteria**: Markets with ≥10 listings but 0 leads in analysis period

### Market Alignment
**Definition**: Comparing lead acquisition patterns with listing inventory.  
**Goal**: Ensure marketing investment matches market opportunity

## Product & Platform Terms

### Realtor.com
**Definition**: Core platform - website and mobile apps (primary top-of-funnel).  
**Role**: Main consumer-facing property search platform

### Homefinder
**Definition**: Strategic incubation platform for high-risk, high-reward strategies.  
**Purpose**: Pilot initiatives like VBB for generating high-quality RCC leads without disrupting main platform

### RCC (Ready Connect Concierge)
**Definition**: Success-based referral product.  
**Business Model**: Commission on closed deals

### New-Com and Moving.com

## Data & Analytics Terms

### Lead Quality
**Definition**: Downstream value or conversion likelihood of a lead.  
**Proxy Metrics**:
- Price segment analysis
- Multiple property views in session
- Time spent on property pages
- Return visits within 7 days
- Contact attempts (phone, email)

### Volume-Weighted Performance
**Definition**: Metrics adjusted for campaign spend/volume.  
**Usage**: Prevents small campaigns from skewing overall metrics  
**Formula**: `SUM(spend * lead_count) / SUM(lead_count)`

### Price Segments
**Definition**: Categorization of properties by list price:
- Under $200K
- $200K-$400K
- $400K-$600K
- $600K-$1M
- $1M+

### Underperforming Campaign Criteria
A campaign/ad group is considered underperforming if:
1. **High CPL**: Cost per lead > 1.5x channel median
2. **Low Volume**: <10 leads in past 30 days despite significant spend (>$1K)
3. **Declining Trend**: 30% YoY or QoQ decline in lead volume
4. **Quality Issues**: Median lead price < 0.5x channel median

### Clickstream Analysis
**Definition**: Tracking user journey from discovery to lead submission.  
**Components**: Sessions from SRP to lead, drop-off points, conversion rates by step

### SRP (Search Results Page)
**Definition**: Page displaying property search results.  
**Funnel Stage**: Top of conversion funnel

### PDP (Property Detail Page)
**Definition**: Individual property listing page.  
**Funnel Stage**: Middle of conversion funnel

### LDP (Listing Detail Page)
**Definition**: Individual property listing page (alternate term for PDP).  
**Funnel Stage**: Middle of conversion funnel  
**Usage**: More commonly used on the buy side

### CPI (Cost Per Install)
**Definition**: Cost to acquire a mobile app installation.  
**Usage**: Mobile marketing efficiency metric

## Analytical & Reporting Terms

### Date Range Abbreviations
- **L7D**: Last 7 days
- **L30D**: Last 30 days
- **MTD**: Month to date
- **QTD**: Quarter to date
- **YTD**: Year to date
- **YoY**: Year over year
- **QoQ**: Quarter over quarter

### Anomaly Detection
**Definition**: Identification of unusual patterns in data:
- **Spend Anomalies**: Days >3 standard deviations from 30-day mean
- **Lead Volume Anomalies**: Day-over-day change >50% or week-over-week >30%

### Null Handling
**Best Practices**:
- Use `NULLIF` when dividing to prevent errors
- Use `COALESCE` for default values
- Filter out nulls in critical fields
